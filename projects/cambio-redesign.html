Cambio Autodelen App Redesign

From accessibility issues to business impact: transforming the registration experience to protect market share in Belgium's competitive car-sharing landscape

Role: UX/UI Designer • Timeline: April 2025 • Type: Mobile App Redesign

The Challenge

The Cambio Autodelen app redesign began with a comprehensive UX research phase that revealed critical insights about user experience and business impact. Despite Cambio's strong position in the Belgian car-sharing market with competitive pricing and an established user base, the app faced significant challenges that threatened its competitive edge.

The existing interface suffered from accessibility issues, including inadequate touch targets, unclear visual hierarchy, and weak brand identity. The registration process was particularly problematic, causing user frustration and potential drop-offs. In an increasingly competitive car-sharing landscape, these UX weaknesses risked losing customers to more modern, streamlined alternatives.

Project Goals

Streamline Registration

Simplify the sign-up process to reduce friction and drop-off rates, making it faster and easier for new users to join Cambio

Strengthen Brand Identity

Create a consistent, recognizable visual identity throughout the app that reinforces Cambio's market position

Improve Accessibility

Address touch target sizes, visual hierarchy, and other accessibility issues to ensure the app works for all users

Localized Experience

Provide multiple language options for Belgium's multilingual market, enabling faster, more comfortable registration

UX & Accessibility Issues

Inadequate Touch Targets

Touch targets fell below the recommended 44x44pt minimum, making it difficult for users to accurately tap buttons and form fields—especially problematic for users with motor impairments or those using the app while on the move.

Medium Accessibility

Lack of Visual Hierarchy

The absence of clear visual hierarchy left users confused about where to focus their attention. Inconsistent spacing and typography created a cluttered, unprofessional appearance that reduced user confidence in the service.

High UX

Weak Brand Identity

The app's visual identity didn't reflect Cambio's brand values or differentiate it from competitors. Generic styling and lack of brand colors made the experience feel disconnected from the Cambio brand users knew from marketing materials, reducing brand recognition and trust.

High Brand

Complex Registration Process

The sign-up process required too many steps with unclear instructions, leading to user frustration and abandoned registrations. Users reported confusion about required information, creating a significant barrier to customer acquisition.

Critical UX

No Multilingual Support

In Belgium's multilingual market (Dutch, French, English), forcing users to navigate registration in a single language increased cognitive load and error rates, particularly for non-native speakers.

High UX

The Redesign Approach

The redesign phase focused on addressing the issues uncovered during user research with targeted, user-centered solutions:

Faster Sign-Up with itsme®

We integrated itsme®, Belgium's trusted digital identity platform, allowing users to sign up in seconds using their existing verified identity. This eliminated lengthy form filling and reduced registration time from several minutes to under 30 seconds, while also ensuring identity verification—a critical requirement for car-sharing services.

Clearer Communication Through UX Writing

Every button label, form field, and instruction was rewritten with clarity and user understanding in mind. Ambiguous phrases were replaced with clear, action-oriented language that guided users confidently through each step.

Multilingual Support for Belgium's Market

Recognizing Belgium's multilingual reality, we introduced clear language selection options (Dutch, French, and English) presented upfront. This localized approach ensures users can complete registration in their preferred language, reducing cognitive load and errors.

Strengthened Visual Brand Identity

The redesign introduced Cambio's brand colors, typography, and visual style consistently throughout the app. This creates a cohesive experience that builds brand recognition and trust while differentiating Cambio from competitors.

Accessibility-First Design

All interactive elements now meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards with appropriate touch target sizes (minimum 44x44pt), clear visual hierarchy, sufficient color contrast, and proper spacing. The design accommodates users with varying abilities and device sizes.

Business Impact

By streamlining the sign-up process and enhancing the overall user experience, this redesign aims to expand Cambio's customer base and maintain its competitive edge in Belgium's car-sharing market.

The design leverages Cambio's existing strengths—competitive pricing, a well-established Belgian user base, and trusted brand reputation—while directly addressing critical weaknesses like the outdated interface and complex onboarding process.

Expected outcomes include:

  • Reduced registration drop-off rates through simplified onboarding and itsme® integration
  • Increased customer acquisition by removing barriers to entry and improving first impressions
  • Enhanced brand perception through consistent, professional design that reinforces trust
  • Improved competitive position by matching or exceeding modern car-sharing app standards
  • Better market penetration across Belgium's linguistic communities through proper localization

In a market where users have multiple car-sharing options, the improved user experience becomes a key differentiator that protects market share and drives growth.

Impact & Key Learnings

This project demonstrated the critical connection between UX design and business outcomes. What began as an accessibility improvement initiative evolved into a comprehensive redesign that addresses both user needs and business goals.

Key Takeaways

  • Small friction points have big business impact: Issues like unclear buttons or extra form fields can significantly affect conversion rates and customer acquisition
  • Localization matters: In multilingual markets, providing native language options isn't just nice-to-have—it's essential for reducing cognitive load and building trust
  • Brand consistency builds trust: A cohesive visual identity throughout the user journey reinforces professionalism and reliability
  • Modern authentication methods add value: Integration with services like itsme® simultaneously improves user experience and meets security requirements
  • Accessibility benefits everyone: Designing for accessibility doesn't just help users with disabilities—it creates a better experience for all users

The redesign positions Cambio to protect and grow its market share in Belgium's competitive car-sharing landscape by removing barriers to entry and creating a modern, trustworthy digital experience.
